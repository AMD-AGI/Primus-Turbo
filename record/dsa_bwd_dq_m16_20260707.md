# FlyDSL bwd dQ M=16 kernel — shared gather + ds_read_tr (2026-07-07)

> Ported the tr16 forward win (M=16 16x16x32 + shared gather + ds_read_tr) to the
> backward dQ kernel, which was the entire bwd bottleneck. Result: bwd dQ 2.9x
> faster, total bwd H128 26->15 ms.

## Diagnosis: dQ kernel was the whole bwd cost
flydsl bwd = 3 phases: (1) native FlyDSL dQ kernel, (2) dKV-intermediate GEMM
(shared Triton), (3) dKV CSR scatter (shared Triton). rocprofv3 kernel-trace:
- OLD dsa_bwd_dq_kernel = **25532 us/call** (M=32, 32x32x16, THREE live accumulators
  s_acc+dp_acc+16xdq_accs -> vgpr_count=512 both pools SATURATED, spill=140-196).
- dkv-intermediate 4810, dkv-gather 1029. dQ was ~96% of bwd.

## Fix: dsa_bwd_dq_m16_kernel.py (built from the tr16 shared-gather base)
Same structure as tr16 fwd, with:
- S = Q.K^T AND dP = dO.K^T: two 16x16x32 MFMAs sharing the kv A-operand (dO is a
  2nd stationary operand loaded like Q).
- softmax REPLACED by elementwise: P = exp2(scale*log2e*S - lse*log2e) (lse sink-
  inclusive, precomputed); dS = P*(dP - delta)*scale. No online softmax, no m/l carry.
- PV REPLACED by dQ += dS @ K (K==V shared tile, same ds_read_tr pipeline; dS is the
  B-operand, staged to the LDS p-region exactly like tr16's p).
- Stores dS, P to [T,H,TOPK] HBM for the (unchanged) dKV kernels.
- SHARED gather: kv gathered once per workgroup into shared LDS, S/dP A-operand read
  from LDS (kills redundant per-wave HBM gather) — the tr16 win.
Wired: PRIMUS_DSA_FLYDSL_BWD_DQ_M16=1 in dsa_bwd.py (default OFF), gate H%16==0 & TOPK%32==0.

## Result (MI355X, real adapter, sink on)
dQ kernel: 25532 -> **8779 us/call (2.9x)**. VGPR 512->256, spill 196->55, agpr 256->0.
| shape | bwd OLD | bwd M16 dq | triton_v2 | gluon_v2 | M16 vs triton | M16 vs gluon |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| H128 K512  | 26.2 ms | **14.9 ms** | 9.25 | 8.40 | 0.62x | 0.56x |
| H64  K512  |  8.8 ms | **9.0 ms**  | 6.75 | 6.86 | 0.75x | 0.76x |
| H128 K2048 | 65.3 ms | **34.6 ms** | 26.4 | 24.2 | 0.76x | 0.70x |
dq SNR vs triton: 80 dB (H128 K512), 51-53 dB (H64, K2048) — all correct. 138 tests pass.
(H64 flat: its dQ was already small; the M=32 K2048/H128 blowup was the pathological case.)

## Remaining bwd bottleneck (for a future session)
New breakdown (H128 K512): dq 8779 + dkv-intermediate 3454 + dkv-gather 700 us.
dq still biggest but no longer dominant. Next levers:
- dq still spills 55 VGPR (dq_accs 128 + s/dp accs + kv operands). occ=1. A 2-buffer
  or Q-in-LDS variant could push occ 2 (tr16 showed occ2 didn't help fwd, but bwd has
  more compute/tile so may differ).
- dkv-intermediate (Triton, shared) is now ~25% of bwd — porting it to FlyDSL MFMA
  is the next structural target if chasing gluon's 24 ms.
